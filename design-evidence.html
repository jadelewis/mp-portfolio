Accessibility: I wanted to make my website inclusive and accessible for people to understand and use. So, I used alt-text to describe an image for visually impaired people and took in consideration of how-to layout my page and which fonts to use which would be the easiest to understand which was a sans safric font. Microsoft is a company who takes accessibility seriously and is a good company to inspire too while designing a website.

“The interactions we design with technology depend heavily on what we can see, hear, say, and touch. Assuming all those senses and abilities are fully enabled all the time creates the potential to ignore much of the range of humanity” (Microsoft 2017)

The persona spectrum is used by Microsoft to understand and have empathy for different peoples struggles and to see if one design could help people with different difficulties, for example if a person has one arm, a person with a temporary injury or a parent holding a baby can also receive help from that design. The persona network also extends to family and friends of a person with a disability so it is important I could create a website that various people can use. Designing a website that is accessible for people creates more emotional content, reduced friction and increased access
